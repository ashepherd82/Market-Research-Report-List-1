<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The microbial fuel cell (MFC) market can be categorized into two types: mediator microbial fuel cells and mediator-free microbial fuel cells. In mediator MFCs, a chemical mediator is used to facilitate the electron transfer between the microorganisms and the electrode. This mediator acts as an intermediary and enhances the cell's energy production. On the other hand, mediator-free MFCs do not rely on any chemical mediator and instead directly transfer the electrons produced by microorganisms to the electrode surface. Both types of MFCs have their unique advantages and applications in various industries.</p></p>
