<p><p>Ripening culture market types include mesophilic cultures and thermophilic cultures. Mesophilic cultures are used for the fermentation of cheeses, such as camembert and blue cheese, at temperatures below 30°C. They contain bacteria that produce carbon dioxide and flavor compounds during cheese maturation. On the other hand, thermophilic cultures are used for cheeses like cheddar and mozzarella, which require higher temperatures above 40°C. These cultures contain bacteria that contribute to the texture and flavor formation in the cheese. Both types of cultures play a crucial role in the ripening process, enhancing the taste and quality of various cheese varieties.</p></p>
