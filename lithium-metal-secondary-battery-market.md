<p><p>The Lithium-Metal Secondary Battery market consists of two major types: Li/Intercalant Cathode and Li/Sulfur market. In Li/Intercalant Cathode batteries, lithium is inserted and extracted from an intercalation compound, while in Li/Sulfur batteries, the cathode contains sulfur. Li/Intercalant Cathode batteries are known for their high energy density and long cycle life, making them suitable for electronic devices and electric vehicles. On the other hand, Li/Sulfur batteries offer even higher energy density but face challenges related to sulfur dissolution and low electrical conductivity. These batteries have potential applications in long-range electric vehicles.</p></p>
