<p><p>Intermittent Pneumatic Compression (IPC) systems are medical devices used to improve blood circulation and prevent blood clots in patients. These systems consist of inflatable sleeves or boots that are wrapped around the limbs and connected to a pneumatic pump. The pump inflates and deflates the sleeves or boots in a cyclical manner to mimic the natural muscle pump action of the legs or arms. This helps in promoting the flow of blood and lymphatic fluids through the veins and reduces the risk of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and other circulatory disorders.</p><p>The future outlook for the IPC system market appears promising. The increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, such as diabetes, obesity, and cardiovascular disorders, is expected to drive the demand for IPC systems. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, which is more susceptible to circulatory problems, is also likely to contribute to market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology have led to the development of portable, user-friendly IPC systems, which offer convenience to patients, thus increasing their adoption. The market's growth is further supported by the increasing awareness about DVT and the benefits of IPC systems among healthcare professionals and patients.</p><p>However, certain challenges may impact the market's growth.
