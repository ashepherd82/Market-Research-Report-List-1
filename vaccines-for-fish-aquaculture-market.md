<p><p>Vaccines for fish aquaculture are categorized into three types: oral vaccines, injectable vaccines, and other vaccines. Oral vaccines are administered by mixing them with fish feed or water, which is ingested by the fish. Injectable vaccines are administered through a needle, directly into the muscle or tissue of the fish. Other vaccines include those administered through immersion, where fish are exposed to the vaccine in water, or by applying it topically. Each type of vaccine has its own advantages and is used based on the specific needs and characteristics of the fish species in aquaculture.</p></p>
