<p><p>Solid Film Lubricants (SFL) are dry lubricants that reduce friction and wear between two surfaces. There are three main types of SFL in the market. PTFE Type consists of polytetrafluoroethylene particles that provide excellent lubricity and chemical resistance. Molybdenum Disulfide Type contains molybdenum disulfide particles, offering high load-carrying capacity and high temperature stability. Graphite Type is composed of graphite particles that provide good lubrication in extreme temperatures and under heavy loads. Each type of SFL offers specific benefits, making them suitable for various applications in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and manufacturing.</p></p>
