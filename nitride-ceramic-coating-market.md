<p><p>Nitride ceramic coatings are used to enhance the properties of different materials, offering advantages such as increased hardness, wear and corrosion resistance. There are several types of coating methods available in the market. Direct Vapor Deposition involves the evaporation and condensation of nitride ceramic precursors onto a surface. Physical Vapor Deposition utilizes a vacuum chamber to deposit the coating material. Plasma Spraying involves melting and spraying ceramic powders onto the substrate. Spray Assisted Vapor Deposition combines the advantages of both vapor and spray deposition techniques. Lastly, "Others" include alternative methods that may not fall under the aforementioned categories.</p></p>
