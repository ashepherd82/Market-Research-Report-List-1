<p><p>Commercial Acousto-Optic Tunable Filters (AOTFs) are widely used in various applications such as spectroscopy, telecommunications, and biochemistry. Collinear AOTFs operate with an acoustic wave and an optical wave travelling in the same direction, allowing for high resolution and narrow bandwidth tuning. On the other hand, noncollinear AOTFs use acoustic and optical waves that travel at different angles, offering lower resolution but wider bandwidth tuning. Both types play a crucial role in manipulating light signals, enabling efficient signal processing and filtering in different industries.</p></p>
