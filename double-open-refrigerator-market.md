<p><p>Double-open refrigerators are commonly used in commercial settings for storing and displaying perishable food and beverages. These refrigerators are available in three main market types: direct-cooled, air-cooled, and mixed refrigeration. Direct-cooled refrigerators use natural convection to cool the interior, making them energy-efficient but prone to temperature inconsistencies. Air-cooled refrigerators use fans to circulate cool air, providing more uniform cooling but consuming more energy. Mixed refrigeration combines both direct and air cooling methods to strike a balance between energy efficiency and temperature control, offering a compromise option for commercial establishments.</p></p>
