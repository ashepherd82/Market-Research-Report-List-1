<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Type I infusion therapy pumps typically refer to large-volume infusion pumps that are used to deliver a high volume of fluids, medications, or nutrients into a patient's body over an extended period of time. These pumps are often used in hospitals, clinics, and home care settings to administer drugs or fluids intravenously.</p><p>On the other hand, Type II infusion therapy pumps are usually smaller and more portable devices that are designed for intermittent or low-volume infusion applications. They are commonly used for short-term infusion therapies, such as patient-controlled analgesia, where patients can self-administer pain medication by activating the pump when necessary. These pumps are often used in both inpatient and outpatient settings.</p></p>
