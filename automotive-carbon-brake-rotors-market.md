<p><p>The automotive carbon brake rotors market comprises two main types: carbon-carbon matrix and carbon-ceramic matrix. Carbon-carbon matrix rotors are made by impregnating carbon fibers with a carbon matrix, resulting in a robust, lightweight, and high-performance braking solution. On the other hand, carbon-ceramic matrix rotors are made by incorporating ceramic fibers into a carbon matrix, offering enhanced durability and heat resistance. Both types of rotors are widely used in high-performance automotive applications due to their exceptional braking performance and ability to withstand intense heat and stress.</p></p>
