<p><p>Geotextiles are permeable fabrics made from synthetic materials such as HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene), LLDPE (Linear Low-Density Polyethylene), PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride), FPP (Flexible Polypropylene), or other similar polymers. These materials are designed to be durable, lightweight, and resistant to environmental degradation. Geotextiles are mainly used in civil engineering and construction projects for erosion control, soil stabilization, drainage systems, and filtration applications.</p><p>On the other hand, geomembranes are impermeable barriers made from similar materials like HDPE, LLDPE, PVC, FPP, or others. These materials are more dense and resistant to seepage or leakage, making them ideal for containment and lining purposes. Geomembranes are extensively used to prevent water seepage in applications like landfills, mining operations, dams, and agricultural ponds.</p><p>Both geotextiles and geomembranes play crucial roles in various construction and environmental projects, but their specific types, such as HDPE, LLDPE, PVC, FPP, or others, determine their suitability and performance in different applications.</p></p>
