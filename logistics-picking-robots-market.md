<p><p>The logistics picking robots market can be divided into two types: full-automatic and semi-automatic. In the full-automatic market, robots are programmed to handle the entire picking process without human intervention. They can navigate through warehouses, identify items, and pick them up, maximizing efficiency. On the other hand, the semi-automatic market involves robots working alongside humans. These robots assist workers in picking tasks but still require human guidance and input. Both types aim to improve logistics operations, but full-automatic robots offer more autonomy, while semi-automatic robots enhance human capabilities.</p></p>
