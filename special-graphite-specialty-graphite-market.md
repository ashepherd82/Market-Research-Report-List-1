<p><p>Special graphite, also known as specialty graphite, encompasses various types of graphite materials used in different industries. Isotropic graphite refers to a high-density and isotropic material with excellent thermal properties. Extruded graphite is created by forcing graphite through a die, resulting in a durable and machinable product. Molded graphite is produced by pressing a mixture of graphite powder and binder, often used for intricate shapes. Lastly, the "others" category includes various forms of special graphite such as vibration-molded, expanded, or purified graphite, each tailored for specific applications in industries like aerospace, automotive, and electronics.</p></p>
