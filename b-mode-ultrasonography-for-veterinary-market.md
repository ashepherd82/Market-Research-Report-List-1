<p><p>B mode Ultrasonography, commonly known as ultrasound, is a widely used diagnostic tool in veterinary medicine. It includes various types such as two-dimensional ultrasound, 3D/4D ultrasound, and Doppler ultrasound. Two-dimensional ultrasound provides a cross-sectional view of the examined area, allowing veterinarians to visualize the internal structures. 3D/4D ultrasound offers a more detailed three-dimensional visualization, enabling a more accurate assessment of abnormalities or growth development. Doppler ultrasound measures blood flow, helping veterinarians in assessing cardiovascular conditions or other vascular abnormalities. These different types of ultrasound contribute to better diagnosis and treatment planning for animals.</p></p>
