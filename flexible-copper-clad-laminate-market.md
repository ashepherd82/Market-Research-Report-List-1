<p><p>Flexible copper-clad laminate (FCCL) is used to manufacture flexible printed circuit boards (FPCBs) as it provides superior electrical conductivity and mechanical flexibility. FCCL is produced through various methods, including tape casting, sputtering, electroplating, and laminating. Tape casting involves spreading a copper slurry onto a substrate and drying it to form a copper layer. Sputtering involves depositing copper atoms onto a substrate using plasma. Electroplating utilizes an electrolyte bath to deposit copper ions onto a substrate. Laminating involves bonding copper foil onto a substrate using heat and pressure. These production methods cater to different requirements and applications of the FCCL market.</p></p>
