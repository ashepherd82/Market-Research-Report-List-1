<p><p>Contact level sensors are used to measure the level of liquid or solid materials in tanks or containers. There are several types of contact level sensors available in the market. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to determine the distance to the material surface. Microwave/Radar sensors use radio waves to measure the level. Optical sensors use light beams to detect the material level. Laser sensors use focused laser beams for accurate measurements. Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems (MEMS) sensors use tiny mechanical components to measure the level. Other types of contact level sensors may include float switches or capacitance sensors.</p></p>
