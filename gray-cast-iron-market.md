<p><p>Gray cast iron is a type of iron alloy that contains graphite flakes, giving it its unique gray appearance. It is classified into three market types based on the microstructure present in the material. Ferrite market type refers to gray cast iron with a predominantly ferritic microstructure, which provides good machinability but limited strength and hardness. Ferrite-pearlite market type consists of a mixture of ferrite and pearlite microstructures, offering improved strength and hardness while retaining some machinability. Pearlite market type is composed entirely of pearlite microstructure, providing the highest strength and hardness but reduced machinability.</p></p>
