<p><p>Process spectrometers are devices used to analyze the chemical composition of materials during manufacturing processes. Near infrared (NIR) type spectrometers utilize light in the near-infrared range to determine material properties. Fourier-Transform Infrared (FTIR) type spectrometers use an interferometer to measure infrared light absorption. Raman type spectrometers analyze the scattering of laser light to identify molecular vibrations. Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) type spectrometers use magnetic fields to analyze the magnetic properties of atomic nuclei. Other types may include UV-Vis spectrometers or mass spectrometers, depending on the specific application.</p></p>
