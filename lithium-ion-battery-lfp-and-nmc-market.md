<p><p>Lithium-ion batteries (LFP and NMC) are rechargeable energy storage devices that use lithium ions to move between the cathode and anode during charging and discharging processes. Both LFP and NMC batteries have their unique properties that make them suitable for different applications. LFP batteries are known for their durability, long lifespan, and safety, making them popular in electric buses and stationary energy storage systems. On the other hand, NMC batteries offer high energy density and are widely used in consumer electronic devices and electric vehicles.</p><p>The market analysis suggests that the increasing adoption of electric vehicles is a key driver in the growth of the lithium-ion battery market.
