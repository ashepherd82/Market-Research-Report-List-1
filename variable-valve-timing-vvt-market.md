<p><p>Variable Valve Timing (VVT) is a technology used in internal combustion engines to improve performance and efficiency. Continuous VVT allows for continuous adjustment of valve timing during the engine's operation, providing greater flexibility and control. On the other hand, non-continuous VVT utilizes a stepped approach, meaning the valve timing can only be adjusted to a limited number of predetermined positions. Both market types offer advantages, with continuous VVT providing more precise control while non-continuous VVT offers simpler and potentially more cost-effective solutions for certain applications.</p></p>
