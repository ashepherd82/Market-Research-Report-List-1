<p><p>The three-point hitch cultivator market can be categorized into two types: hanging and semi-mounted. </p><p>1. Hanging Cultivator: This type of cultivator is attached to the three-point hitch at the rear of the tractor, with its frame hanging freely. It is lighter and compact, making it easier to maneuver and navigate around smaller fields or areas with obstacles. Hanging cultivators are typically utilized for light to medium-duty cultivating tasks and are suited for tractors with lower horsepower.</p><p>2. Semi-Mounted Cultivator: Unlike the hanging cultivator, the semi-mounted variant is attached to the three-point hitch but also has additional support through a wheel or wheels at the front. This configuration allows for better weight distribution and stability while working, making it suitable for heavy-duty cultivating tasks. Semi-mounted cultivators are often used in larger fields or for more demanding applications, requiring tractors with higher horsepower capabilities.</p></p>
