<p><p>Organic corrosion inhibitors are chemical compounds used to protect metal surfaces from corrosion. Amines organic corrosion inhibitors are composed of amino groups and work by forming a protective film on the metal surface. Phosphonates organic corrosion inhibitors contain phosphonic acid groups and form a protective layer by chemically bonding with the metal surface. Benzotriazole organic corrosion inhibitors act by adsorbing onto the metal surface and forming a protective layer. The "others" category includes various types of organic corrosion inhibitors that do not fall into the aforementioned categories.</p></p>
