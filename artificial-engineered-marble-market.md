<p><p>Artificial engineered marble, also known as cultured marble, is a composite material made from a combination of crushed natural marble and resin. This mixture is then molded into various shapes and sizes, providing a cost-effective alternative to natural marble. The finished product looks and feels like natural marble but is more durable, easier to maintain, and less expensive.</p><p>The artificial engineered marble market is witnessing significant growth due to its numerous advantages over natural marble. Its easy installation, resistance to stains and scratches, and lower cost make it an attractive choice for various applications such as countertops, flooring, and wall claddings. The rising demand for aesthetically appealing and low-maintenance building materials in the construction industry has also contributed to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, artificial engineered marble offers a wide range of customization options in terms of colors, patterns, and finishes, making it suitable for both residential and commercial projects. <p><p>The artificial engineered marble market includes two main types: solid surface and engineered quartz stone. Solid surface materials are composed of acrylic, mineral fillers, and pigments, offering excellent durability and flexibility. They are commonly used in kitchen countertops, bathroom vanities, and furniture due to their resistance to staining and impact. Engineered quartz stone, on the other hand, consists of natural quartz crystals and polymer resins. It provides a luxurious appearance, high strength, and resistance to heat and scratches, making it popular for various applications in both residential and commercial spaces.</p></p>
