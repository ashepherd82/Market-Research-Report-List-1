<p><p>Conventional RIDT refers to rapid influenza diagnostic tests that rely on conventional methods such as lateral flow immunoassays to detect the presence of flu antigens in the patient's sample. These tests provide results within minutes but may have lower sensitivity and specificity compared to laboratory methods. On the other hand, digital RIDTs utilize advanced technologies like digital imaging systems or smartphone apps to interpret and display the test results digitally. These tests offer greater accuracy and faster result interpretation, allowing for better decision making in point-of-care settings.</p></p>
