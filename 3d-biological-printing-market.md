<p><p>The 3D biological printing market is divided into three types: inkjet-based 3D bioprinting, syringe-based 3D bioprinting, and laser-based 3D bioprinting. Inkjet-based bioprinting works by depositing biological materials layer by layer using inkjet printing technology. Syringe-based bioprinting utilizes a syringe to extrude bio-inks onto a substrate in a controlled manner, enabling the formation of complex tissue structures. Laser-based bioprinting employs laser energy to precisely position cells or cell aggregates, enabling the creation of intricate biological structures. These technologies offer promising applications in the field of regenerative medicine and tissue engineering.</p></p>
