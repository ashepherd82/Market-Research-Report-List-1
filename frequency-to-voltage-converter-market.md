<p><p>The frequency-to-voltage converter market consists of two main types: analog channel and differential channel. </p><p>Analog channel converters are designed to convert the input frequency to a corresponding voltage output using a single channel or pathway. These converters are commonly used in applications where a simple and basic conversion is required. </p><p>On the other hand, differential channel converters operate by measuring the frequency difference between two input channels and producing a voltage output based on this difference. These converters are typically utilized in more complex applications that require more precise and accurate frequency measurements.</p></p>
