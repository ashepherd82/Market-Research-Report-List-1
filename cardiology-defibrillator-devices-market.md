<p><p>Cardiology defibrillator devices refer to medical devices used to treat cardiac arrhythmias, specifically to restore either a normal heartbeat or a viable ventricular rhythm in patients experiencing sudden cardiac arrest. The market for these devices can be categorized into three types. Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillators (ICDs) are surgically implanted devices used for long-term management. External defibrillators are portable devices used by medical professionals to deliver electrical shocks to the heart. Wearable Cardioverter Defibrillators (WCDs) are lightweight devices worn externally and provide continuous monitoring, detection, and treatment for at-risk patients.</p></p>
